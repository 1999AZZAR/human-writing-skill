# AI Writing Signs (Wikipedia Field Guide)

This reference lists common "tells" of AI-generated content to be avoided in professional and human-like documentation.

## 1. Overused Vocabulary ("AI Slang")
Avoid these high-density words that LLMs tend to over-index on:
- **Verbs:** delve, underscore, highlight, enhance, foster, bolster, align with, showcase, garner, embrace.
- **Nouns/Adjectives:** tapestry, testament, nuances, complexities, intricacies, landscape, pivotal, crucial, vibrant, comprehensive, multifaceted, evolving, groundbreaking.
- **Connectors:** Additionally (especially at sentence starts), Moreover, Furthermore, In conclusion, Ultimately.

## 2. Puffy & Promotional Tone
- **Puffery:** Avoid "stands as a testament to," "serves as a reminder," "marking a pivotal moment."
- **Peacocking:** Don't use "nestled in the heart of," "boasts a," "rich cultural heritage," "renowned," "exemplifies a commitment to."
- **Superficial Analysis:** Avoid attaching "-ing" phrases at the end of sentences for fake depth (e.g., "...creating a lively community").

## 3. Structural Formulae
- **Negative Parallelisms:** "Not just X, but also Y," "It's not about A, it's about B."
- **Rule of Three:** Mechanical lists of three adjectives or three short phrases (e.g., "fast, secure, and reliable").
- **Rigid Outlines:** Sections named "Challenges," "Legacy," "Future Outlook" used in a formulaic way.
- **Outline Leads:** Treating broad titles or lists as proper nouns (e.g., "The 'List of X' is a curated compilation...").

## 4. Stylistic Tells
- **Avoiding Copulas:** Overusing "serves as" or "represents" instead of simple "is" or "are."
- **False Ranges:** "From X to Y" where X and Y don't actually form a logical scale (e.g., "from fundamental physics to medicine").
- **Elegant Variation:** Using forced synonyms (e.g., "the protagonist," "the key player") to avoid repeating a name.
- **Title Case Headings:** Capitalizing every word in a heading regardless of standard style.
- **Mechanical Boldface:** Boldfacing every instance of a key term.

## 5. Logic & Attribution
- **Weasel Wording:** "Experts argue," "industry reports suggest," "some critics say" without specific citations.
- **Over-attribution:** Listing sources to prove notability instead of summarizing their content.
- **Social Media Obsession:** "Maintains an active social media presence" (a common AI-ism).
